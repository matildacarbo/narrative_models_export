MAIN_Method

icon: LiCheckSmall

Storyboard elicitation. Set of four stories presented through a sequence of 6 pictures. These stories were designed directly for MAIN. They are controlled for complexity, and cross-cultural appropriateness.

Data collected from children & adults (adults serve as benchmarks for children)
Multiple languages/cultures; monolingual and multilingual

MAIN_Coding schema

icon: LiCheckSmall

Transcription rules are very explicit & precise (see MAIN_Transcription_rules), probably informed by conversation analysis.
Annotation procedure much less so.

  • Description of annotation is given in:
      • Gagarina et al 2012_MAIN p.13: Westby's decision tree shown as guide to code episodes.
    • Gagarina et al 2019_MAIN_Adult p.197-198: This article lists the categories, without describing the process. Multiple coders with high agreement.
    • Lindgren Bohnacker 2022 p.492: This article lists the coding categories, without describing the process.
    • Klopp & Smith 2025 p.181-182: Coding of macrostructure relies on Westby's decision tree. Data labelled as "no sequence" receive an additional label: description, or action. Two coders, with high agreement. No description of process beyond that.
      Started looking for an annotated dataset on MAIN's website. Could continue: https://zenodo.org/records/4724970#.Yf1qqKbMK5d
      They have a standard annotation procedure that produces high agreement between codes. It's practical for their purpose, but flawed theory-wise.

Checked other sources to see if I could find a more recent & comprehensive annotation procedure; I did not find anything of the sort.

  • Gagarina & Bohnacker 2019_MAIN-revised
  • Lindgren et al 2019
  • Full package of MAIN documents on website; Transcription Rules does not touch on annotation. See https://www.leibniz-zas.de/de/publications/schriftenreihe/zaspil/zaspil-63/main-63-download#c2844

Old notes

Examples of score rubric shown in Gagarina et al 2012: 67-86
One score rubric created per story
The score rubric has more or less items depending on the complexity & length of the story.

Here's a rough adaptation:

expression labelled labelling what labelling how (1): category complexity labelling how (2): categories used
? macrostructure 7 categories setting, IST as initiating event, goal, attempt, outcome, IST as reaction, (non-IST) initiating event
? type of internal state terms 4 categories perceptual, physiological, consciousness, emotion

The rubric also tracks:

  • Quantified version of "Structural complexity"
    • Nb of AO sequences
    • Nb of G (no AO) sequences
    • Nb of GA / GO sequence
    • Nb of GAO sequences
  • Nb of internal state terms (total and nb by type)
    When adapting this protocol for labelling data, quantification of structural complexity can flow directly from coding data with macrostructure categories.
