Labov & Waletzky_Method

Interviewer prompts a speaker to narrate a personal experience involving danger/risk of death
Data collected in two settings (with other community members, or without)
From a diverse set of speakers (variable age -- adults only --, race, and place of origin = +-urban).
Single language/culture

Labov & Waletzky_Short description

Labov & Waletzky (1967) developed a model to study narratives; specifically oral narrative by average speakers.
It is the most influential linguistic approach to date (perhaps with more influence in social sciences than linguistics per se). They innovated by studying oral versions of personal experiences narrated by average speakers, whereas previous research studied folk tales, myths, legends... narrated by expert storytellers.

Labov & Waletzky_Short description

Labov & Waletzky_Purpose

  • Context: Prior research modeled narratives with a narrow, idealized dataset (myths, folk stories,... by expert storytellers). Better modeling can be achieved with a wider dataset (wider range of narratives, and of storytellers represented). (p.12)
    • Implicit connection with lang acq (age variation) and variationist description (class, origin, race,... variation)
  • This work models tellings of personal experiences by average speakers from diverse backgrounds. It enriches narrative research by considering new types of narratives, and a different set of storytellers. (p.12)
  • Purpose: Theory building

micro formal Narrative clause "[a narrative clause] cannot be displaced across a temporal juncture without a change in the temporal sequence of the original semantic interpretation" (p.27); "a narrative clause has an unordered displacement set" (p.27)
micro formal Free clause Clause with an ordered displacement set equal to the whole narrative = paraphrase of: "If the displacement set is ordered... [and] if such an ordered set is equal to the narrative as a whole, the clause is a free clause" (p.27); "can range freely through the narrative sequence" (p.22)
micro formal Coordinate clause "Clauses with identical displacement sets may be termed coordinate clauses" (p.23)
micro formal Restricted clause "Such clauses, which are neither free, nor temporally ordered in the strict sense, may be termed restricted clauses." (p.22); clause with an ordered displacement set that is not equal to the whole narrative = paraphrase of p.27
micro formal Narrative heads todo p.28
macro_X functional Orientation optional setting statement, providing, time, place, etc. = paraphrased from: "free clauses... [that] serve to orient the listener in respect to person, place, time, and behavioral situation" (p.32); "free clauses which precede the first narrative clause" (p.32)
macro_X functional Complication "the main body of narrative clauses usually comprises a series of events which may be termed the complication" (p.32); "the complication is regularly terminated by a result" (p.33) (nb result is synonym for resolution)
functional Evaluation "...that part of the narrative which reveals the attitude of the narrator towards the narrative by emphasizing the relative importance of some narrative units as compared to others" (p.37)
macro_X functional Resolution "that portion of the narrative sequence which follows the evaluation" (p.39)
functional Coda optional return to present perspective = paraphrased from "a functional device for returning the verbal perspective to the present moment" (p.39)
